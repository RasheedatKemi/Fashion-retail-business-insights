# Fashion Retail Business Insights

## Project Overview
This project analyzes sales performance for a fashion retail company to uncover business insights that support strategic decision-making. The analysis focuses on identifying top-performing sales channels, geographical strongholds, store efficiency, and opportunities for regional expansion.

## Tools Used
- Excel (Data Cleaning)
- Power BI (Dashboard & Visualization)
- Data Analysis & Business Intelligence

## Key Business Questions
1. Which sales channels generate the highest revenue?
2. Which territories and cities perform best?
3. How does store size affect sales performance?
4. What opportunities exist for improving underperforming regions?

## Key Insights

### 1. Top Performers
- **Dominant Sales Channel:** *Fashions Direct* is the strongest sales channel, contributing the majority of the **$47.34M total sales** and **$71.04M annual sales**.
- **Geographical Stronghold:** *Maryland (MD)* is the top-performing territory with leading cities including **Laurel, Pasadena, and Annapolis**, significantly outperforming other regions.

### 2. Efficiency and Scaling Opportunities
- **High-Yield Store Size:** Stores with **10,000 sq. ft. selling area** generated the highest annual sales of **$32.95M**.
- **Low Performing Store Sizes:**  
  - 65,000 sq. ft stores: **$2.69M**
  - 20,000 sq. ft stores: **$0.50M**
- This suggests diminishing returns from larger store formats.

### 3. Performance Gap
There is a major sales drop between the top territory **Maryland (MD)** and other territories such as **North Carolina (NC)** and **Virginia (VA)**.  
States like **Delaware (DE)** and **Kentucky (KY)** contribute minimal sales.

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Replicate the Maryland Model
Maryland stores are significantly outperforming others.

**Recommendation:**  
Analyze inventory mix, store layout, and marketing strategies used in **Laurel and Pasadena** and replicate this model in weaker territories such as **Tennessee, Kentucky, and Delaware**.

### 2. Optimize Store Size Strategy
Data suggests that **smaller boutique-style stores outperform larger formats**.

**Recommendation:**  
Prioritize opening **10,000 sq. ft high-efficiency stores** instead of larger stores that produce lower returns.

### 3. Investigate Data Integrity
An entry labeled **"Fapshions Direct"** shows unusually low sales.

**Recommendation:**  
Verify whether this is:
- A **data entry error**, or
- A **separate underperforming brand**.

### 4. Regional Expansion Strategy
North Carolina and Virginia are **mid-tier performers** with growth potential.

**Recommendation:**  
Conduct targeted **market expansion and marketing campaigns** in these territories to create a secondary stronghold outside Maryland.

## Project Outcome
This analysis provides data-driven recommendations to improve sales performance, optimize store footprint, and guide regional expansion strategy for the fashion retail business.
